Suburban ACTs and college readiness

The table below is sortable on a number of measures:

  • School: Name of the school. Click the school name and you'll be taken to that school's page on reportcards.dailyherald.com where you can dive deeper into grades, demographics and more.
  • ACT 2013: Average ACT score in 2013 for students in the high school
  • Change: Percentage-point change in the average score from 2012
  • % ACT 21: Percentage of students who scored a composite ACT score of 21 or more, which is about the average composite of the four benchmark scores below.
  • % English 18: Percentage of students who scored 18 or more on the English subject area of the test.
  • % Math 22: Percentage of students who scored 22 or more on the Math subject area of the test.
  • % Reading 22: Percentage of students who scored 22 or more on the Reading portion of the test.
  • % Science 23: Percentage of students who scored 23 or more on the Science portion of the test.

What are ACT benchmarks?

A student scoring at least the benchmark score in the subject areas listed is considered by the ACT to have a 50% chance of scoring a B or higher in the corresponding first-year college course.
